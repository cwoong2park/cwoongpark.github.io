About Me

How can we make more inclusive and efficient urban space using digital technology?

We have witnessed that digital technology has changed how people behave in cities, and cities have also changed their forms to adopt the technology. Urban computing, which is a comprehensive research field for computer-based approaches to urban study, is essential to determining where we have to invest in resources and services. I strongly believe that digital technology will play a key role in explaining and solving urban problems.
In particular, urban inequality and inefficiency derived from physical and spatial restrictions can be overcome by digital technology. I'm interested in figuring out how to overcome physical restrictions via digital technology and applying it to urban planning and design.

Spatial Influence of ICT - Digitally Accessible Space

ICT has become a considerable determinant to depict urban space and people's travel behavior. To figure out the empirical impact of ICT on urban space, I suggested the notion of Digitally Accessible Space, which provides online reserved visitors with significant priorities. In conclusion, DAS utilizes ICT including online reservation and mobile device to overcome the unprofitable market location.

    Enhancing the Disaster Resilience of Declining City

    Since declining city is more likely to be vulnerable to natural disaster, the risk assessment of the site is required with multi-dimensional approach. Specifically, this project focused on the risk assessment of a small area, analyzing based on 1m scale grid. I addressed climate data which represent the hazard factor based on IPCC 2014 framework and obtained 3D point cloud data using drone & LiDAR.

    Overtourism Detection by Human Mobility

    Planning strategy to prevent overtourism requires data-driven analysis for human mobility. Bukchon in Jongno, a residential area with traditional buildings and aesthetic landspace, is the most representative site suffering from overtourism. I analyzed the human mobility pattern of visitors obtained by CCTV and telecommunication data in a street unit using PostGRESQL and python.

    AI-driven Urban Block Generator

    Simulating Water Flow on Facade

    I conducted research for the simulation of water flow pattern on facade during my internship in VS-A.kr. A real experiment was conducted to understand the water flow pattern depending on the material and angle. Based on the experiment, the pattern was parametrized by CFD simulation (Realflow) and visualized by 3D modeling tool (C#, Rhino3D & Grasshopper).

  • Park, C. W., Park, J., Kim, N., & Kim, Y. (2018). Modeling water flow on Façade. Automation in Construction, 93, 265-279.
